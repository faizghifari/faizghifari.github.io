Bismillah

*365 days ago, at a similar time, I was lying down on a bed in a hotel while watching a Formula 1 GP. It is not an ordinary night. Besides me, there was this cutie little woman who was on a brink of her sleep.*

.

*You are right. That day, this cutie little woman officially became my long-life partner. It was an awkward night, to be honest, as usually expected from a newlywed here in my country.*

.

*Today, 365 days after I took an oath in front of Allah, her parents, families, and audiences, I am currently sitting down on my office chair writing down these words while being separated from her almost 200 KM away.* *An oath* *such that I promise to not only be her husband* *but to guide her, teach her, lead her, protect her, and swear for that almost everything about her life is now also being officially* *my life too.*

.

*I spent my time now to recall back on what happened these past 12 months. It is really a roller-coaster one. From life in a rented apartment in the middle of an industrial complex, me not having any salary at all because of the pandemic, went to Bandung to track our previous lovely college life, exploring foods although we have 180 degrees of different tastes, all the big-small conflicts that we had along the way, and now living abroad pursuing each of our own master degrees while not being able to live together.*

.

*Unique.*

*I guess, is the best word to describe all of my experiences with her. It is something that I never felt before, and for sure it is part of my growth in adultery life. Of course, I do not say that I am always happy all the time. There are times when we had conflicts, angry with each other. But our bond on top of a sacred oath is what keeps us together and strives through all those hard times. Just like this day, our anniversary day, when I am forced to sleep in our apartment in Gung-dong, Daejeon, and she sleeps in the POSTECH dormitory in Pohang, separated by around 2 hours of travel at least.*

.

*It is not easy, to live as a foreigner and have to say goodbye to your partner 1-3 times each month at a train station or a bus terminal. I remember after saying that bye phrase, I just get gloomy all day because of not getting used to being alone again.* *Seeing an empty bedroom, while before there was her smile and her funny chats that went all around the place.* *Life is hard, indeed, but I believe what does not break our bond will only make it stronger.*

.

*In last words, I knew on our important day, we can only be together just for merely less than 12 hours*. *However, I pray in the name* *of Allah, that something like this will just happen only this once, and just get better year by year if our destiny permits.*

*For my beloved wife, a.a.r.a.i, for the **first of many**,** happy anniversary dear love**.* *Let’s cherish and be grateful for what we have, and stay strong together regardless of what conditions we are going to face.*

.

***Love you, always.***
